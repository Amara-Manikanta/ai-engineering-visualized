Why Subwords?
Splitting into full words creates a huge vocabulary (millions of entries). Characters are too granular. Subwords (BPE, WordPiece) balance efficiency — ~50,000 token vocabulary covers all English text.
Token ≠ Word
"gravity" becomes ["grav", "ity"] — two tokens. "GPT-4" might be ["G", "PT", "-", "4"]. Code and non-English text often tokenize less efficiently, using more tokens per word.
Context Window
LLMs have a maximum context length measured in tokens (e.g., 128K for GPT-4o). Once you exceed this, the oldest tokens are dropped. This is why very long conversations lose early context.

What is d_model?
The embedding dimension. GPT-2 uses 768, GPT-3 uses 12,288, GPT-4 reportedly 4096+. Larger d_model = more expressive representations but more compute and memory.
Position Encoding
Transformers have no inherent notion of order. Positional encodings (or RoPE, ALiBi) are added to embeddings to tell the model the order of tokens in the sequence.
Learned Representations
The embedding matrix is learned during training. Semantically similar tokens end up with geometrically close vectors — the geometry of meaning that enables semantic reasoning.

Steps 5 & 6

🎲 LM Head + Sampling Strategy

After the transformer stack, a final linear layer maps from d_model back to vocabulary size (~128K tokens). Softmax converts logits to probabilities. Then a sampling strategy picks the next token.

Always pick the single highest-probability token. Fast, deterministic, but often repetitive and boring. Temperature = 0.

Restrict sampling to the K most likely tokens (e.g., K=50), then sample from that pool. Balances quality and diversity.

Include tokens until their cumulative probability reaches P (e.g., 0.9). Adapts dynamically — fewer tokens when confident, more when uncertain.

Step 7 — Speed Trick

🚀 Speculative Decoding

A clever trick to make LLM inference 2-4x faster: a small Draft Model quickly generates K candidate tokens, then the large Target Model verifies them all in a single forward pass.

Why It Works
LLMs verify tokens much faster than they generate them (batch processing is more efficient than sequential). Each verification pass is essentially free compared to generation.
Speed Gains
2-4x inference speedup in practice. Used in production by Google (Gemini), Anthropic, and OpenAI. The smaller the draft model gap, the better the acceptance rate.
Draft Model Choice
The draft model must be from the same model family for high acceptance rates. E.g., Gemini Nano drafts for Gemini Ultra, or Llama 3.2-1B drafts for Llama 3-70B.
