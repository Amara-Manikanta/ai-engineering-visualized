Spell Corrector

Algorithms that identify misspelled words and suggest the most likely correct words based on edit distance and probabilities.

How it works

Edit Distance (Levenshtein)

The foundation of spell correction. It calculates the minimum number of single-character edits (insertions, deletions, substitutions) needed to change one word into another.

Candidate Generation

Generating all possible words that are 1 or 2 edit distances away from the misspelled word.

Language Model Probability

Scoring the generated candidates using word frequencies from a large corpus. The candidate with the highest probability is chosen as the correction.
