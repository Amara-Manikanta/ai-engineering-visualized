NLP

Natural Language Processing

Techniques and algorithms for processing, analyzing, and generating human language text.

Lexical Processing

The foundational step in NLP involves breaking down text into basic units and normalizing them.

Tokenization

Splitting text into individual words or sub-words (tokens).

Stemming & Lemmatization

Reducing words to their root forms. Stemming is rule-based and aggressive, while Lemmatization uses dictionary context.

Stopwords

Filtering out highly common words (like 'the', 'is', 'in') that carry little semantic value.

Edit Distance

Measuring similarity between strings by counting the minimum operations to transform one into the other (e.g., Levenshtein distance).

Text Representation

Machine learning models require numerical inputs, so text must be converted into vectors.

Bag of Words (BoW)

Represents text as a frequency count of words, ignoring grammar and word order.

TF-IDF

Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency. Weighs words by how frequently they appear in a document relative to the entire corpus.

Applications & Advanced Concepts

Core tasks built on top of basic NLP processing.

Spam Detection

Using text representation and classification algorithms to identify unwanted messages in datasets like SMSSpamCollection.

Spell Correction

Utilizing edit distance and phonetic algorithms like Soundex to suggest corrections for misspelled words.

Beam Search

A heuristic search algorithm used in sequence generation tasks to explore the most likely sequences of words.

Syntactic Processing

Analyzing the grammatical structure of sentences to understand relationships between words.
