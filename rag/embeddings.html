Part 5

Embeddings

The mathematical foundation of semantic search.

What are Embeddings?

Embeddings are dense numerical vectors that represent the semantic meaning of text. Words with similar meanings are mapped to points close to each other in a high-dimensional mathematical space.

Dense vs Sparse Embeddings

  • Dense Embeddings: (e.g. OpenAI, Cohere) Arrays of non-zero floating point numbers representing abstract meaning.
  • Sparse Embeddings: (e.g. SPLADE, TF-IDF) Huge arrays where most numbers are zero, focusing on exact keyword frequencies.

Models & Dimensions

Different models output different dimensions. OpenAI's text-embedding-3-large supports up to 3072 dimensions, while smaller open-source models like all-MiniLM-L6-v2 output 384 dimensions. Higher dimensions capture more nuance but are slower to search and cost more to store.

Similarity Metrics

To compare how similar two embeddings are, Vector DBs use mathematical formulas:

  • Cosine Similarity: Measures the angle between two vectors. (Most common for text).
  • Dot Product: Measures angle and magnitude. (Preferred if embeddings are normalized).
  • Euclidean Distance (L2): Measures the straight-line distance between the points.
